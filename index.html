Analysis: Progress – Signals – Interventions

Progress

High buy-in and engagement across program: 89% CDG adoption rate, 73% RML adoption rate
15 agencies/builders/TA PMs (45%) forecasting to achieve >90% circular performance
10 agencies/builders/TA PMs (30%) forecasting >50% performance
Tools (CDG, RML, education) valued and widely adopted
96% CDG satisfaction rating (Rating 4: 42%, Rating 5: 54%)
87% RML satisfaction rating (Rating 4: 52%, Rating 5: 34%)
Top performers demonstrate excellence is achievable - 45% forecast achieving circular status by AO26
TA internal asset tracking capability saw a step-change to track assets for AO26

Signals

Positive feedback and high adoption rates misaligned with qualitative feedback. Responses indicate a potential overstatement of circular ambition, evidenced by:
Sustainability statements without transparency: 27 of 29 participants reference sustainability commitments, only 3 publish these publicly
Ambitious forecasts without enabling systems: 8 organisations project 20%+ improvement, without supporting systems/processes
Limited resourcing and investment: Circular initiatives are often under-resourced, constraining delivery and scalability
Inconsistencies between adoption and practice: Adoption of CDG and RML is not reflected in qualitative responses or operational capability
Waste management and asset tracking remain systemic gaps that cannot be closed in 6 months without significant resources
22-point deficit in waste management between TA and Agencies - likely indicative of lack of reliable waste and asset data to demonstrate circular outcomes
Circular design knowledge retention risk due to attrition and annual employment cycles

Interventions for Consideration

1. Develop TA's response to key themes: Budget, waste management, and temporary materials
2. Expert review of the RML for temporary materials
3. TA approval for long-term design: Update CDG to align including precinct waste targets and continue communications and education. Celebrate stories and provide recognition
4. Post event reporting to encourage reality check on transformation timeframes, communicate challenges, embrace setbacks as learning, celebrate wins
5. Rethink waste measurement and connection to teams
6. Standardise simple systems for asset tracking for knowledge and asset retention
7. Zone in on mid-performers with greatest propensity for growth/change
8. Small agency support package - offer simplified compliance requirements
9. Understand the role of procurement in enabling circular practices

Next Steps

01. Distribute to Circular Design Advocates for review/ input
02. Consider what feedback is material (prioritised) to be addressed (how and when)
03. We have limited performance visibility. Forecasted improvements lack clear mechanisms to track actual performance against projections — consider how to encourage actual outcomes in post event reporting
04. Establish how far-reaching the Circular program awareness has been during event, that is, the gaps to include in future (agencies, builders, suppliers, designers)
⚠️ The circular program is at a turning point. Constraints need to be addressed to avoid initiatives being abandoned.
